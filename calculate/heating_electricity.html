Heating¶

Heating emissions are computed based on the consumption (typically in kWh) and the emission factors for a specified fuel type.

co2calculator.calculate.calc_co2_heating(consumption: float, fuel_type: str, unit: str = None, area_share: float = 1.0) → float[source]¶

Function to compute heating emissions

Parameters:

  • consumption (float) – energy consumption

  • fuel_type (str) – fuel type used for heating [coal, district_heating, electricity, gas, heat_pump_air, heat_pump_ground, liquid_gas, oil, pellet, solar, woodchips]

  • unit (str) – unit of energy consumption [kwh, kg, l, m^3]

  • area_share (float) – share of building area used by research group

Returns:

total emissions of heating energy consumption

Return type:

Kilogram

Per default, the expected unit is kWh. For some fuel types, the consumption may also be specified using different units, e.g., litres of oil or kg of wood chips. In these cases, it is possible to specify the unit. The consumption will then be converted from the specified unit to kWh, based on common conversion factors:

The parameter area_share accounts for the fact, that the heating energy consumption may often only be known for an entire building, while a working group just occupies parts of the buildin. In this case, the (approximate) share of the building floor space, that is occupied by the working group can be provided. The area_share must be between 0.0 and 1.0 and is 1.0 by default.

Electricity¶

Electricity emissions are computed based on the consumption (in kWh) and the emission factors for a specified energy mix or energy source.

co2calculator.calculate.calc_co2_electricity(consumption: float, fuel_type: str = None, energy_share: float = 1) → float[source]¶

Function to compute electricity emissions

Parameters:

  • consumption (float) – energy consumption

  • fuel_type (str) – energy (mix) used for electricity [german_energy_mix, solar]

  • energy_share (float) – the research group’s approximate share of the total electricity energy consumption

Returns:

total emissions of electricity energy consumption

Return type:

Kilogram
